# Trading 101 — just enough to read an SMT call

**Long vs short.** Going *long* means you profit if the price rises. Going *short* means you
profit if it falls. SMT can do both, symmetrically.

**Conviction & sitting out.** A good system doesn't trade constantly. When no signal is strong
enough, the best move is **WAIT** — no trade, no fees, no risk. SMT has a conviction floor for
exactly this.

**Risk, not just reward.** Every trade can go wrong. SMT shows a risk score and sizes for the
personas that *disagree* with the call, so you see the other side too.

**Fees matter.** Every round-trip trade costs a fee. SMT will not take a trade whose expected
profit doesn't clear its fees — overtrading quietly kills returns.

**Why "explainable" matters.** If you can't see *why* a system acted, you can't learn from it or
trust it. SMT's whole design is to make the reasoning legible to a beginner.
